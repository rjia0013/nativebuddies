Get Involved

Know How You Can Contribute

Be the Change

Birds are highly mobile and spread around a vast area, hence individual efforts are required in determining their population. You can get involved doing bird watching and bird counting in your backyard and observe the rate of its population in your area.
In the year 2020, ten times more residents than the previous year participated in citizen science activities in Victoria and the data turned vital for bird conservation measures.

Bird Watching & Bird Counting

Birdwatching is simply the observation of birds around you through naked eye, using visual enhancement(binocular,webcam) or listening to their call.
Bird counting is the observation of birds for a definite period and location and then recording the count of them.
Find out how

01

Get out, choose the best time

Select a clear day. Get out in the backyard. Be quiet and avoid sudden movements. Be patient. Best time to see birds is early mornings when the sun comes up as they are very active.

02

Choose your outfit

Wear dark outfits or tones that match the background. Avoid bright colours like white as they enhance the appearance of movement in birds.

03

Identify the bird

Look out for the birds. Listen to their voice. Aid your visual and vocal identification from the page ‘Impacted Birds’ on this website.

04

Know the Habitat

Look in trees and shrubs. Look on the lawn. Peek into the nest box. Observe perching stick, bird feeders and bird bath.

05

Try ‘Pishing’

Make small squeaky noises. Try ‘pish’ by blowing air through closed teeth. Birds attracted to such noise and pop out to investigate.

06

Helpful Gadgets

Invest in a good binocular to have a clear vision and not disturb the birds. Keep a camera handy and click pictures to observe later. Keep a journal to record sightings.

Be a citizen scientist
