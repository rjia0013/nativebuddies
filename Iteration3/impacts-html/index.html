Pollination

Pollination is the process of transferring of pollen between male and female parts of a plant, leading to fertilization and seed formation.
Ecosystem Impact
Human Impact
How Birds Help
Ecosystem Impact
  • When the population of native birds decreases, especially honey eaters, it results in a reduced activity of pollination on plants.
  • Low pollination leads to low plant fecundity, resulting in low production of fruits & seeds for dispersal & propagation.
  • Plant population decreases and genetic diversity is unbalanced, leading to extinction
Human Impact
  • Fruits and vegetables, which add diversity to the human diet and provide essential nutrients is affected.
  • Seven of the nine crops that provide at least 50% of the vitamin C available to the human diet globally depend partially or entirely on animal pollination for the production of fruits or seeds.
How Birds Help
  • Native birds, like honey eaters, are major pollinators of around 30 genera of diverse and abundant plants in Australia.
  • Pollen gets placed on forehead, face, chin feathers or beak of honey eaters and lorikeets when feeding on nectar from flowers.
  • Birds carry appreciable quantities of pollen and visit more flowers making them the predominant pollinators.

Seed Dispersal

Seed dispersal is the process of birds dispersing the seeds through on their body, in its beak/claw, during feeding and through droppings.
Ecosystem Impact
Human Impact
How Birds Help
Ecosystem Impact
  • A decrease in native bird population leads to a rate of decrease in the seed dispersal phenomenon.
  • Low seed dispersal results in seeds growing into plants near the parent, resulting in overcrowding, and competition for water, minerals & sunlight, followed by death of plants
  • Low plant regeneration to extended areas results in decline of rate of forest expansion. Dying of forests leads to increased global warming, impacting Earth’s ecosystem.
Human Impact
  • Reduction in seed dispersal results in decline of forests due to low plant regeneration.
  • Low rate of forest formation leads to oxygen depletion & climate change.
  • Climate change leads to extreme heat waves, rising sea-levels, changes in precipitation resulting in flooding and droughts, intense hurricanes, and degraded air quality, affect directly and indirectly the physical, social, and psychological health of humans.
How Birds Help
  • Birds eat the fruits from plants and defecate them, thereby releasing the seed at a different place for plants to develop.
  • A mutual coexistence environment where birds benefit from the fruits and plants benefit from seeds moved to other germination sites.
  • The seed comes out of the bird with faeces, a natural fertilizer, helping in the regeneration. Seed Coat are sometimes scratched which assists easy germination.

Pest Control

Pest control is the process of birds naturally eradicating pests that endangers plants by feeding them and spreading infection among pests.
Ecosystem Impact
Human Impact
How Birds Help
Ecosystem Impact

Native bird’s population decrease can lead to increase in the pests.

  • Uncontrolled pests can damage the plants forever.
  • In the absence of birds, pests need to be controlled using pesticides, harming the plant and fruit.
  • Pesticides can spread around land, water & air, affecting the entire ecosystem.
Human Impact
  • Insects & pests increase due to decrease in bird population.
  • Humans then start using pesticides to control pests.
  • Eating pesticide treated fruits and vegetables can be fatal to the human body in the long run causing health effects like cancer.
How Birds Help

Birds play a major role in eradicating pests in a natural, effective and cost-efficient way.

  • Birds feed on pests that damage the plants.
  • Mobility of birds helps them to move as a group to infected areas to feed on pests.
  • Birds spread viral infection among pest, thereby totally eradicating them.
