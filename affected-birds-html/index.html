Affected Birds

Tips To Identify Commonly Affected Native Birds

Explore
Fairy-Wren
Physical

14 cm long and weighs 813 g. Males slightly larger than females. The average tail length is 5.9 cm. 10 species found in Australia.

Color Pattern

Males have bright blue forehead, ear coverts, mantle, and tail, with a black mask and black or dark blue throat. Females are a plain fawn color with a lighter underbelly and a fawn.

Behaviour

Carnivores, mainly insectivores. Feed on insects such as ants, grasshoppers, shield bugs, flies, weevils and various larvae as well as small quantities of seeds, flowers, and fruit.

Habitat

Seen where dense cover and low shrubs occur. Common in urban parks and gardens, and can be seen in small social groups.

Kookaburra
Physical

Large head, prominent brown eyes, long and robust bill. 4147 cm in length. 2 species found in Australia.

Color Pattern

Laughing kookaburras have dark brown wing plumage and a white head and underside. Black upper bill with dark brown eye stripes across the face.

Behavior

Carnivores and diet includes small birds, frogs, insects, small mammals and lizards like geckos. Known to catch venomous snakes much larger than themselves.

Habitat

Native to woodlands and open forests in Australia, perching in large trees and nest in cavities of tree trunks and branches. Very common in suburban and urban areas.

Australian Parrot
Physical

Strong, curved bill, an upright stance, strong legs, and clawed zygodactyl feet. 56 species found around Australia.

Color Pattern

Mostly vividly coloured, some are multi-coloured. Males and females are hard to identify based on color.

Behavior

Parrots are omnivores, eat both meat and vegetation. Diet mostly contains nuts, flowers, fruit, buds, seeds (favourite food) and insects.

Habitat

Humid and heavily forested upland regions of Australian eastern part, including eucalyptus wooded areas in and directly adjacent to subtropical and temperate rainforest. Very common in suburban areas.

Honey Eater

Physical

Small-medium size, body length of 1216 centimetres, a wingspan of 1823 centimetres and an average weight of 911 grams. Around 75 species found in Australia

Color Pattern

Brownish, with yellow-olive panels in the tail and wing, and a yellow tuft behind the eye.

Behavior

Mainly feeds on the foliage and flowers in the canopy of trees and shrubs. Mainly feed on nectar from flowers using brush tipped tongue. Also feed onInsects, pollen, berries and manna

Habitat

Tropical, subtropical, and temperate rainforests, eucalyptus forest, monsoonal forest, woodlands with eucalyptus and coastal heaths. Several species are common in parks and gardens.
